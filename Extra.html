What is AI?


AI is short for Artificial Intelligence. An umbrella term for applications that perform complex tasks that once required human input. One of the most exciting aspects of AI is the fact that it is able to perform tasks that were once considered to be exclusive to the domain of human creativity. For example, AI can now generate art, music, and even creative writing. Despite the creative expression of AI, it is also being used to solve most of the world's biggest problems. From climate change and the efficient use of energy to healthcare system and transportation. The vast amount of data that is being analyzed is being saved into the membrane of the Artificial Intelligence's brain. As well as the patterns and trends that may not be common knowledge to humans. The system is able to output accurate predictions and more informed decisions. AI is the literal personification of Intelligence itself.


How Does it Work?


The SAS Institute states that AI is trained and works "by combining large amounts of data with fast, literative processing and intelligent algorithms, allowing the software to learn automatically from patterns or features in the data." An algorithm able of an approach to continuously improve a concept, design, idea, or product is fed samples of human made examples of the task given to replicate the process and product. Attempting to closely achieve the similarity of the samples given. The networks of an AI is modeled after the brain of a human being, these networks are designed to learn, adapt and predict the processed data that it is given. This training mechanism is special because once an AI is used to the tasks it is given, it can then be used to perform a wide range of tasks, from speech recognition and language translation to image and video analysis. These tasks would now be able to happen on it's own without needing human input.
